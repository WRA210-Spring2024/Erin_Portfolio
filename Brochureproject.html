Turbulence Brochure

To develop my technical writing skills, I created an informative brochure introducing and clarifying the properties of air turbulence. This document covers why rough air occurs at high altitudes and how airplane pilots can avoid going through the radar-indicated rough air.

Below, I have broken down the three sections on the infographic, expanding on the purpose and intentionality behind the information and images as it relates to the turbulence justification.

Section 1: What is Turbulence and Why Does it Occur?

The first section serves as a "beginner" guide to the process of turbulence to give backgrond context on why passengers might feel the abrupt movements on the aircraft.

Section 2: The Tropopause Layer

The middle section provides reasonong behind the discontinuation of cloud formation called, the tropopause and why flying above it plays in favor for flight paths.

Section 3: Turbulence Avoidance

Lastly, my third section elabortaes on the firt two parts of the infographic, explaining how pilots can try to stay clear of turbulence while flying around strong winds and above the troposphere.
